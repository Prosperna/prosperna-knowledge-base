# P1 | Email Notifications | Low Stock Notifications BRD

### **Executive Summary:**

The _Low Stock Notifications_ feature empowers Prosperna merchants to proactively manage their inventory by receiving real-time alerts when products approach or reach zero quantity. It reduces lost sales opportunities caused by unmonitored stock depletion and helps merchants restock critical items before they run out. The system sends both in-app and email notifications, ensuring merchants are informed through their preferred channels. By combining automated tracking, configurable thresholds, and plan-based eligibility, this feature enhances operational efficiency and improves customer satisfaction through better product availability.

### **Background:**

Prosperna is an eCommerce platform that lets their merchants sell products and services through their online store website. It recognizes the significance of inventory visibility and real-time product availability in maintaining smooth operations and customer trust. Merchants often lose potential sales due to delayed awareness of low or depleted stock levels, especially when managing multiple store locations or products with variants. This feature was developed to automate inventory monitoring and provide timely alerts, allowing merchants to act quickly and maintain optimal stock levels across all store locations.

### **Business Objectives:**

- **Prevent Lost Sales Opportunities** — Ensure merchants are promptly notified when products are running low or have sold out to minimize downtime in product availability.
- **Enhance Operational Efficiency** — Automate stock-level monitoring across multiple locations and variants to reduce manual inventory checks.
- **Improve Merchant Experience** — Provide a seamless and non-intrusive alert system through in-app and email notifications, keeping merchants informed in real time.
- **Support Tier-Based Differentiation** — Offer this feature exclusively to Pro and Premium plan merchants to add value to higher-tier subscriptions.
- **Reduce Support Workload** — Minimize merchant complaints or confusion related to unexpected stockouts through proactive communication.
- **Increase Platform Engagement** — Encourage merchants to log in more frequently and take timely inventory actions through in-app notifications.

### **Scope of Solution:**

- Allow merchants (Pro and Premium plans) to enable or disable low-stock notifications in **Settings > Product Settings > Inventory Alerts**.
- Default threshold value set to **10**, configurable by merchant.
- Minimum allowed threshold = **0**, negative values not accepted.
- System automatically detects stock changes after checkout events or stock deductions.
- Email notifications are **batched per minute** with a **24-hour cooldown per variant** to prevent spam.
- In-app notifications are **per product**, sent in real-time through the **existing WebSocket framework**.
- In-app notifications redirect directly to the concerned product’s **Edit Product Page**.
- Separate notification logic and templates for **Low Stock** and **Out of Stock**.
- Automatic feature disablement upon merchant downgrade from Pro/Premium plan, and silent re-enablement upon upgrade if previously active.
- All notifications (email + in-app) are suspended while the feature is disabled.
- Admin users retain full visibility for auditing merchant stock behavior and notification logs.

### **Out-of-scope:**

- Manual merchant edits to product quantities do **not** trigger notifications.
- SMS or push notifications are **not included** in this release.
- No merchant-level customization of email or in-app notification templates.
- No reporting or analytics dashboard for stock notification history.
- Notifications are available **only** to the main merchant account, not sub-users.
- Advanced AI-based stock prediction or reorder automation is excluded from scope.

### **Business Requirements:**

- The system must validate merchant’s plan before allowing access to the Inventory Alerts feature.
- When a product’s stock equals or falls below the defined threshold, the system must trigger a **Low Stock** notification.
- When a product’s stock reaches zero, the system must trigger an **Out of Stock** notification.
- The system must group low-stock and out-of-stock products separately when sending batched email notifications.
- The system must use WebSockets to send per-product in-app notifications in real time.
- The in-app notification must redirect to the product’s **Edit Product Page**.
- The email notifications must follow a **1-minute debounce window** to batch related product alerts.
- Each product variant must follow a **24-hour cooldown period** to prevent repetitive notifications.
- The system must automatically disable this feature upon plan downgrade to Free or Plus, and silently re-enable it upon upgrade if previously active.
- The system must halt all active email and in-app notifications when the feature is disabled.
- The feature must maintain consistent functionality across merchants with multiple store locations.
- Default field behavior: threshold = 10, minimum = 0, negative input disallowed.

#### **Notes (architecture):**

- `NotificationAPI` is the central controller: validates plan, checks notification flag, checks cooldown and rate-limits, writes to DB, emits WebSocket events, enqueues email batch entries.
- `WebSocketLayer` pushes per-product notifications for merchant sessions.
- `NotificationQueue` groups events (per merchant, per type) for 1-minute batching for email send.
- `SubscriptionService` must expose plan check and emit webhooks for plan-change events (downgrade/upgrade).
- `NotificationDB` holds notifications, email batches, cooldown records, and rate-limit counters.

# Non-Functional Requirements Specification (NFRS)

#### 1\. System Performance & Behavior

- Notification triggers are based on post-order checkout stock deductions.
- Email notifications are **batched every 1 minute**; in-app notifications are **sent per product in near real time (≤5s)**.
- **24-hour cooldown** applies per product variant per notification type.
- **Manual stock edits** do not trigger notifications.
- **Disabling** the feature stops all queued notifications within 3 seconds.
- **Error handling:** Retries failed sends up to 3 times; unprocessed messages logged to notification service.
- **Trigger order:**
  1. Checkout reduces stock
  2. System checks thresholds
  3. Products queued for email batch
  4. WebSocket emits real-time in-app notification

#### 2\. Scalability & Load Handling

- Supports up to **20,000 merchants/hour** with consistent latency.
- Email rate limit: **12 per hour** (6 low-stock, 6 out-of-stock combined).
- In-app notifications: up to **100 events/merchant/hour**, ≤5KB payload each.
- Notification queues (SQS/Kafka/RabbitMQ) must support **100,000 concurrent events**.
- Aggregation per merchant and per notification type.

#### 3\. Reliability & Data Integrity

- Availability target: **99.9% uptime**.
- Notifications rely on **committed inventory data**, not cached.
- Failed emails retried 3×; failed WebSocket emits retried once, then stored as unread.
- Event logs retained for **30 days** for audit.

#### 4\. Security & Access Control

- Trigger logic executes under authenticated sessions or service tokens.
- Only **Pro/Premium** merchants authorized to use the feature.
- Payloads contain product data only (no customer or payment data).
- All API and WebSocket traffic secured via **TLS 1.2+**.

#### 5\. Maintainability & Extensibility

- Implemented as a **modular Notification Manager submodule** (supports future SMS/push).
- Key parameters (threshold, cooldown, rate limits) configurable via environment variables.
- System logs every trigger, send, and suppression event to centralized monitoring (e.g., CloudWatch/Datadog).
- Plan changes (upgrade/downgrade) automatically toggle the feature via billing webhooks.

#### 6\. User Experience Consistency

- All emails and in-app messages must follow Prosperna’s tone and notification design.
- **Silent enable/disable:** no toast, modal, or banner displayed for plan-triggered changes.
- In-app notification click redirects to the **Edit Product Page** via `/inventory/edit/:productId`.

#### 7\. Data & Integration Requirements

- **Stored fields:** `{ merchantId, productId, variantId, qty, type, channel, timestamp, status }`.
- Records older than **30 days auto-deleted** via scheduled cleanup.
- Integrations:
  - Inventory Service (triggers event)
  - Order Service (provides transaction data)
  - Notification Manager (email + in-app)
  - Subscription Service (handles plan eligibility)
  - Email Gateway & WebSocket Layer (delivery)

# Test Plan

| **Test Type**                     | **Description**                                                                                                             | **Primary Focus**                              |
| --------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| **Unit Testing**                  | Validate core functions such as stock threshold evaluation, cooldown enforcement, rate-limiting, and notification triggers. | Ensures accurate backend logic.                |
| **Integration Testing**           | Verify the interaction between order service, inventory service, notification service, WebSocket layer, and email gateway.  | Confirms seamless inter-service communication. |
| **Functional Testing**            | Validate the merchant-side configuration flow (enable/disable, save threshold, etc.) and ensure UI behaves as expected.     | Ensures end-to-end merchant functionality.     |
| **Regression Testing**            | Confirm that introducing this feature does not affect existing inventory management or order flows.                         | Maintains platform stability.                  |
| **Performance Testing**           | Measure system behavior under high order volume and notification concurrency.                                               | Ensures scalability and rate-limit adherence.  |
| **User Acceptance Testing (UAT)** | Validate overall user experience from merchant perspective, including in-app and email delivery.                            | Confirms usability and feature completeness.   |
| **Plan Eligibility Testing**      | Test various scenarios of upgrade/downgrade behavior and automatic enabling/disabling logic.                                | Ensures correct feature gating based on plan.  |
| **Security Testing**              | Verify that notifications, emails, and WebSocket messages respect tenant isolation and access control.                      | Prevents data leakage between merchants.       |
